Q
What exactly is Value Engineering, and how is it different from a regular design review?

A regular design review checks whether your circuit works correctly — does the schematic have errors? Does the PCB route properly?

Value Engineering goes far beyond that. It asks: can this design be manufactured at scale, reliably, at the lowest possible cost, without supply-chain risk? It covers three dimensions simultaneously:

  • Technical perfection — electrical correctness, signal/power integrity, thermal, EMC
  • Cost optimization — BOM reduction, component rationalization, volume pricing
  • Supply-chain resilience — alternate sourcing, lifecycle management, vendor qualification

The result is a design that doesn't just work on the bench — it works at 10,000 units/month with predictable cost and yield.

Q
My prototype is already working. Why do I need this before mass production?

A working prototype and a production-ready design are very different things. Common issues that only appear at scale:

  • Yield problems — soldering defects due to poor DFM, components too close together, missing fiducials
  • Supply disruptions — single-source ICs going on allocation, 26-week lead times discovered after placing the order
  • Cost surprises — BOM costs 40% higher than expected at volume because no one negotiated pricing or checked alternates
  • Field failures — thermal issues, ESD susceptibility, or derating violations that only surface under real-world conditions

Industry data shows that 70–80% of product lifecycle cost is locked in during the design phase. Fixing these issues after tooling is 10–100x more expensive than fixing them now.

Q
What do you need from us to get started?

We need your current design package. Typically this includes:

  • Schematic files — native EDA format (Altium, KiCad, OrCAD) or PDF
  • PCB layout files — native EDA format, Gerber/ODB++, or at minimum a PDF with all layers
  • Bill of Materials (BOM) — Excel/CSV with part numbers, quantities, manufacturer names
  • Product specification — what does the product do, operating conditions, target environment
  • Target production volumes — even a rough estimate (100/month vs. 10,000/month) changes the optimization strategy

That's it. We handle everything from there and come back with actionable reports.

Q
How much BOM cost reduction can we realistically expect?

It depends on the current state of the design, but based on our experience across multiple products:

  • 15–25% reduction is typical for designs that were prototype-optimized but never production-optimized
  • 25–40% reduction is achievable when the original design used premium/evaluation components without considering volume alternatives
  • 10–15% reduction for designs that have already gone through one round of cost optimization

The savings come from multiple sources: passive value consolidation (fewer unique part numbers), package size optimization, volume pricing negotiation, switching to pin-compatible lower-cost alternates, and eliminating over-specified components.

We provide a projected savings table broken down by volume (1K, 10K, 100K units) so you can see the exact ROI.

Q
Will you change our schematic or PCB design, or just review it?

Our standard engagement is a review + recommendation model:

  • We thoroughly review your schematic, BOM, and PCB layout
  • We deliver detailed reports with every issue classified by severity (Critical / High / Medium / Low)
  • Each issue includes the specific recommended fix — not vague suggestions, but exact component values, routing changes, or layout modifications

Your team can then implement the changes, or we can implement them for you as a follow-on engagement. Either way, you retain full ownership of all design files and reports.

Q
Can you also help us find and qualify a contract manufacturer (CM)?

Yes. Phase 4 (Supply-Chain Strategy) specifically covers this:

  • We shortlist 2–3 PCB fabrication houses (domestic India + international backup) matched to your stackup and volume
  • We shortlist 2–3 PCBA assembly houses with SMT capability that matches your component density and package types
  • We audit their capabilities against your specific design requirements

The Phase 5 Production Readiness Package is specifically designed so that any qualified CM can build your product independently with the documentation we provide — Gerbers, BOM, assembly drawings, pick-and-place files, test procedures, and QC plan.

Q
How long does the full 5-phase engagement take?

For a single product board, the typical timeline is:

  • Phase 1–3 (Schematic + BOM + PCB review): 3–4 weeks in parallel
  • Phase 4 (Supply-chain strategy): 2–3 weeks, can overlap with Phase 1–3
  • Phase 5 (Production readiness package): 2–3 weeks after design changes are finalized

Total: 6–8 weeks from kickoff to a complete production-ready package. This is significantly faster than the typical 3–4 prototype re-spin cycles that companies experience without VE (which can take 6–12 months).

If you need a specific phase urgently (e.g., just the schematic review before your next PCB order), we can prioritize and deliver that within 1–2 weeks.

Q
Can we engage for just one or two phases instead of all five?

Absolutely. Each phase is designed to deliver standalone value. Common configurations:

  • Quick VE — Phase 1 + 2 only (Schematic + BOM). Best if you just need design validation and cost optimization before your next board spin.
  • DFM Focus — Phase 3 only (PCB Layout + DFM). Best if your schematic is solid but you want to ensure the layout is production-ready.
  • Full Production Prep — All 5 phases. Best if you're moving from prototype to volume production and want everything locked down.

We recommend starting with Phases 1–3 at minimum, since schematic, BOM, and PCB issues are deeply interconnected. But it's your call — we tailor to your needs.

Q
What standards and methodologies do you follow for the review?

Our review process is checklist-driven and grounded in industry standards:

  • IPC-A-610 — Acceptability of Electronic Assemblies (for soldering and assembly quality criteria)
  • IPC-2221/2222 — PCB design standards (trace widths, spacing, via sizing)
  • IEC 61000-4-x — EMC and ESD immunity standards
  • UL/CE requirements — Safety and compliance considerations for your target markets
  • Component derating guidelines — Per JEDEC and manufacturer recommendations

Every finding in our reports references the applicable standard, so your team (or any third party) can independently verify our recommendations.
